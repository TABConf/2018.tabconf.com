Jeff Tucker

Editorial Director, AIER.org

Jeff Tucker

Editorial Director, AIER.org

Bio: Jeffrey Tucker is an early adopter of cryptocurrency who was out in front with the intellectual defense of the theory, practice, and future of synthetic monies and assets. He was one of the first writers in the tradition of Austrian economics to explain the viability of the currency as an alternative to national monies and the realization of F.A. Hayek’s vision of the denationalization of money and choice in currency. He has written more than 200 articles on the topic in the scholarly and popular press.

Keynote: People think it is an investment. A speculation. Maybe a bubble. But actually the distributed ledger allows humankind to achieve something that has never before been possible. All the rest is an application.

Shawn Wilkerson

Founder and CSO, Storj Labs Inc.

Shawn Wilkerson

Founder and CSO, Storj Labs Inc.

Bio: Shawn Wilkinson is the cofounder and chief strategy officer at Storj Labs where he oversees company strategy, vision and architecture of the Storj network. He also works directly with community members to understand the requirements of developers, users and farmers to ensure their needs align with product development. He graduated from Morehouse College with Bachelor of Science in Computer Science.

Talk: In the modern era, data runs the world and the majority of your data sits with a handful of cloud providers, giving them detailed visibility into your life and your business. Decentralized cloud storage aims to balance the scales in favor of consumers by providing a platform that is private at the foundational level, meaning no one can access data except the data owner. In this session, Shawn Wilkinson with Storj Labs will share how decentralized cloud storage powered by blockchain technology is creating an ecosystem that is more secure, private and more affordable than traditional cloud storage solutions.

Peter Rehm

CPA

Peter Rehm

CPA

Bio: Peter is a CPA who has worked and consulted within the banking industry as a corporate controller, financial reporting manager and senior systems technology development consultant at a succession of large firms including Bank of America, PNC Banks, The Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. (FDIC) and mortgage industry giant Federal National Mortgage Association (FNMA). After graduating from Rutgers Business School, he started his career working with Big 4 firm Ernst & Young and where he spent a fair amount of time preparing and reviewing corporate and personal tax returns. After Ernst & Young, while working for a small bank he was asked to set up and manage its Fedwire wire transfer system, a smaller version of the widely used Swift money transfer system. At that time he came to appreciate how important value transfer systems are to the world economy. Peter believes that cryptocurrencies are the democratic evolutionary descendants of Swift and looks forward to advancements in blockchain technologies.

Talk: Taxation of Blockchain-based Cryptocurrencies - The arrival of cryptocurrency to more widespread global use has resulted in questions of how the United States in 2017 will tax blockchain oriented cryptocurrency transactions that 1.) Are new to the business lexicon (forks/ airdrops/ mining), 2.) Are covered by existing laws (capital gains and losses/ barter), or 3.) Have possibly been incorrectly reported in prior years by taxpayers (Use of Code Section 1031 as previously applied to cryptocurrency swaps).
